About

I'm on the ground in China. You don't have to be.

Most overseas buyers will never set foot in the factories that make their products. That's a problem — because what you can't see can cost you. That's why I'm here.

I live in China. I visit factories in person. I check samples with my own hands. And I treat every client's order like it's my own money on the line.

Why I built this

The problem with "just find the factory directly"

I see many buyers looking for suppliers, and most of the time the first idea is: find the factory, get the lowest price, cut out the middleman. But in real sourcing — especially when you are still small — the real problem is often not just "can I find a factory?"

› Will the factory care about your small order?

› Will they check details before production?

› Will they reply clearly and patiently?

› Will they handle samples properly?

› Will they help combine goods from different suppliers?

› Will they care if your first shipment goes wrong?

Most factories are built for production, not for hand-holding small buyers. That is not because they are bad. It is just not their business model.

Why a good middleman exists — and why I built this

Big sourcing companies can also be good. They may have better systems and more experience. But if you are a small client, you may not get much attention there either.

So in many cases, the most realistic option is not "factory only" or "big company only". It is finding the right middle person or small team who fits your stage.

This is exactly why I built chinapartnerhub.com. Not to be another sourcing agent who forwards emails. But to be the person on the ground who actually takes responsibility for the outcome.

Who I work best with — and who I don't

I work best with small to mid-sized brands, e-commerce sellers, and startups — people doing $5,000 to $100,000 orders who can't afford a bad supplier decision.

I don't work with everyone. If your only concern is the absolute lowest price, I'm not the right partner.

If you value quality, reliability, and having someone who tells you the truth — even when it's not what you want to hear — let's talk.
