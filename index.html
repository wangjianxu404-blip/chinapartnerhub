The Reality

Most people sourcing from China are one bad supplier away from a disaster.

Alibaba looks easy. A few messages, some sample photos, and a price that seems too good to pass up. But behind that profile picture could be a trading company with no factory, using stolen photos, shipping goods that look nothing like the samples. We've seen it happen hundreds of times.

Real Story

$1,400 or $1,200 — do you really know who you're paying?

A client was choosing between two suppliers quoting $1,460 and $1,400 for the same machine. Same photos. Similar claims. The cheaper one looked like the obvious choice.

We checked both. Registered addresses? Residential buildings. Both were trading companies — not manufacturers. The client wasn't comparing two factories. He was comparing two layers of middlemen.

We traced the supply chain one step further. The real factory was offering the same machine at $1,200. The difference wasn't $60 — it was $200 of unnecessary markup with zero production control.

"The most expensive mistake in sourcing isn't overpaying. It's not knowing who you're actually doing business with."
