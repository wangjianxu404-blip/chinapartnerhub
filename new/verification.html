What we actually review

A useful verification starts with real supplier materials, not only a company name.

The strongest first-pass checks usually combine a supplier link, product context, quote logic, and at least one piece of visual or document proof. China Partner Hub can work from partial information, but stronger inputs produce a sharper go / no-go recommendation.

Basic supplier identifiers

Alibaba link, website, company name, contact card, WhatsApp, or email signature. This is the minimum layer needed to start checking role and consistency.

Commercial documents

Catalog PDF, quote sheet, MOQ, packaging spec, payment terms, or product list. These help separate a serious supplier from a vague sales front.

Visual production proof

Workshop photos, sample photos, packaging shots, or short factory video. Visuals show whether the supplier context matches the story being sold.

Main risk question

Is the supplier real, are the prices believable, can they handle the category, or are they just good at talking? The review works best when the buyer names the decision in front of them.

Factory context

The workshop environment should support the supplier story, not contradict it.

Print, stitching, and packing details often reveal more than a polished storefront page.

Sensitive files do not need to be public. Buyers can send catalogs, quotes, screenshots, or short clips by email or WhatsApp after payment.
