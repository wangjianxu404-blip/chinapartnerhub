China Tea Sourcing Guide: Which Provinces & Categories Work for US/EU Buyers

A practical reference for buyers considering tea sourcing from China — covering Yunnan's distinctive high-value teas plus Zhejiang and Anhui for standardized export production.

Why tea sourcing requires a category-first approach

Tea is not one product. It's dozens of categories, each with different growing regions, processing methods, quality grades, and export dynamics. A buyer who approaches tea sourcing with "I want to import Chinese tea" without understanding these distinctions will struggle to get meaningful quotes — or worse, receive samples that don't match what their market expects.

This guide focuses on three provinces that together cover the spectrum from premium specialty tea to standardized export production. Use it as a reference when planning factory visits or discussing requirements with suppliers.

Yunnan: The heavyweight for distinctive, high-value tea

Yunnan province is China's most recognizable tea origin. Its combination of ancient tea mountains, unique large-leaf varietals, and strong regional identity makes it the go-to for buyers who need product story as much as product quality.

CategoryRepresentative ProductsBest For
Pu'er Tea Raw Pu'er, Ripe Pu'er, Ancient Tree Tea, Compressed Tea Premium gifts, collectible tea, Chinese community markets, tea culture channels
Dianhong Black Tea Yunnan large-leaf black tea, Fengqing Dianhong Mainstream US/EU tea market, gift boxes, tea bags, blended tea
Yunnan Green Tea Large-leaf green tea, sun-dried green tea Price-sensitive orders, tea bags, beverage raw materials
White Tea / Moonlight White Yunnan white tea, Moonlight White Boutique tea shops, e-commerce, small-batch high-margin products
Herbal / Flavored Tea Tea blended with rose, jasmine, dried tangerine peel Brand customization, gift boxes, functional tea concepts

Top 3 most recommended Yunnan categories for US/EU buyers: Pu'er Tea + Dianhong Black Tea + Yunnan White Tea. These three give you the strongest combination of regional identity, international recognition, and margin potential.

What makes Yunnan tea sourcing different

Yunnan tea sourcing often involves visiting not just processing factories, but also tea mountains and primary processing sites. The supply chain is more distributed — farmers pick and initially process, then factories refine, package, and export. This means supplier visits in Yunnan are more complex but also more valuable: you can trace the product from mountain to finished package, which is a powerful story for premium positioning.

Zhejiang & Anhui: Standardized export production at scale

While Yunnan excels at distinctive and high-value teas, Zhejiang and Anhui provinces handle the volume side of China's tea exports. If your market needs consistent green tea for tea bags, bulk black tea for blending, or premium gift-box products with reliable supply, these provinces are where you should look.

ProvinceMain Tea CategoriesExport Strengths
Zhejiang Green tea, Longjing tea, Gunpowder tea, Mee tea Strong US/EU green tea demand, tea bags, traditional bulk tea exports
Anhui Green tea, Huangshan Maofeng, Keemun black tea Premium green tea, black tea, gift-box products

How to position your tea sourcing strategy

The most effective approach for US/EU buyers is often a dual-province strategy:

  • › Yunnan for your distinctive, high-margin, story-driven products — the teas that differentiate your brand
  • › Zhejiang + Anhui for your standardized, high-volume, consistent-supply products — the teas that fill your tea bags and gift boxes

This combination means you're not just presenting one factory visit to buyers — you're presenting a broader China tea supply chain solution. That's a much stronger position than "I found a tea supplier on Alibaba."

Practical notes for tea sourcing visits

When planning factory visits for tea, keep these in mind:

  • › Seasonality matters. Tea is harvested in specific windows. Visiting during harvest season lets you see the full process. Visiting off-season means limited production visibility.
  • › Compliance is category-specific. Herbal and flavored teas face more complex regulatory requirements for US/EU import than pure teas. Confirm compliance before committing to samples.
  • › MOQ varies dramatically. Premium Pu'er from ancient trees might have a 5kg MOQ. Standard green tea for tea bags might start at 500kg. Match the category to your volume expectations.
